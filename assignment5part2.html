Finishing my Lamp

Using my sketches from earlier, I finalized my lamp design in Rhino. I ended up going with the crystal ball shaped shade and flat base.

I printed several tests of the spherical shade and found that I was struggling with adhesion and gaps in my print despite using a brim and re-leveling my bed several times. I figured the new clear filament I was using was part of the problem, so I bumped the temperature of the nozzle and the bed up and this helped with the clarity of the printed filament and solved some of the bed adhesion challenge. However I was still struggling and found that gaps were occurring at the top of my sphere. So, I made the layer height smaller, increased the top layers, and made the top print in a concentric pattern. This solved the rest of my adhesion and gapping issues, and I ended up with a clean spherical shade.

I also struggled to create the bulb holder bit of my lamp, and now realize that I needed to be using boolean commands to ensure that my mesh was closed and valid for Cura. Now that I have realized this I can go back into Rhino and reprint my base to work with the classic light bulb as well. However, I am glad that I struggled with this nit because I ended up creating a more simple base that fits with my wireless light bulb/string lights and these light sources bring a nice gentle glow to the final design and use less power.
